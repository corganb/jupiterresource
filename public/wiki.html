Overview

Jupiter is the fifth planet and largest in the Solar System, a gas giant more than twice the mass of all other planets combined. It has 95+ known moons including the four Galilean satellites (Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto), a faint ring system, and the iconic Great Red Spot - a persistent storm larger than Earth.

Active missions

Pioneer 10 / 11 (NASA)
Flybys 1973/1974
First spacecraft to visit Jupiter. Characterized magnetosphere and radiation belts.
Voyager 1 / 2 (NASA)
Flybys 1979
Discovered Jupiter's rings, detailed imagery of Galilean moons, captured Io volcanism.
Galileo (NASA)
Orbit 1995-2003
First Jupiter orbiter. Deployed an atmospheric probe. Studied moons in detail.
Cassini (NASA/ESA)
Flyby 2000
En route to Saturn. Joint observations with Galileo.
Juno (NASA)
Orbit since 2016
Polar-orbiting spacecraft studying interior, magnetic field, and auroras. Captures JunoCam imagery.
Europa Clipper (NASA)
En route, arrival 2030
Launched 2024. Will perform ~50 flybys of Europa to assess habitability of its subsurface ocean.
JUICE (ESA)
En route, arrival 2031
Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer. Will orbit Ganymede after Jupiter tour. First mission to orbit another planet's moon.

Notable features

Great Red Spot
Persistent anticyclonic storm, has been observed for ~400 years. Shrinking in recent decades.
Galilean moons
Io (volcanic), Europa (ice-covered ocean world), Ganymede (largest moon in Solar System), Callisto (most heavily cratered).
Ring system
Faint, discovered 1979 by Voyager 1. Composed of dust from moon impacts.
Radiation belts
Intense radiation environment. Damages spacecraft electronics; missions use shielded vaults.
